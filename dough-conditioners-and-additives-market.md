<p><p>Dough conditioners and additives enhance the quality and performance of dough in baking. The market is divided into two primary types: powders and fluids. Powdered conditioners typically include ingredients like enzymes, emulsifiers, and dough relaxers, offering ease of storage and extended shelf life. Fluid conditioners, often more concentrated, provide immediate blending with dough, improving texture and fermentation processes. Both forms serve to optimize dough elasticity, shelf stability, and overall product quality, catering to diverse baking needs in the industry.</p></p>
